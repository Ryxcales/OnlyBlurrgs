Phasediver: The Day the Plurrg arrived

A Tale of Nervura, Blurrg, and the Crawgs

Tazavesh shimmered with neon lights and echoing voices, markets stacked upon markets, riddled with smugglers, shady traders, and questionable meats on sticks. Nervura’s Reshii Wraps trailed like a shadow behind her; beside her, Blurrg padded silently, his snout up in the air.
He knew there were snacks nearby. focused croak

They were on a mission! Food supplies meant for The Oasis outposts had gone missing, and evidence pointed toward the brokers and their… dubious trading schemes.
They had already interrogated a chattering customs officer, bribed a masked broker with questionable cheese, and blended into a crowd of dancing 100% legally imported ethereal birds.

Finally a lead. A crate-stamped ledger. A half-eaten blood sausage. Footprints that smelled like fraud. Nervura smirked.
“Got you.”
Blurrg bonked the ground in agreement, stealthily. Mostly.
Together, they tracked the smuggler through twisting platforms, shimmering portals, and past vendors yelling about limited-time soul bargains. They cornered him behind a set of crates, and Nervura’s swiftly set out her traps.
A few spearstrikes, a couple of tactical headbonks, and the stolen food was theirs again.
The perfect duo—just like the day they first met.

But then… reality wobbled.
The air shimmered—purple, cold, whispering.
A tear in space ripped itself open with a deep, humming fwooomph.
Nervura readied her spear immediately.
Blurrg planted his tiny legs and growled at the freshly opened tear.
The smuggler shrieked, scrambling backward.
Something small and curled shot out of the portal like a cosmic potato:

plop
It bounced once.
Twice.
Then landed at Nervura’s boots.
It blinked.
Purple glow. Big eyes.
Void crackles gently fizzing off its horns.

“…A purple Blurrg?” Nervura whispered.
She looked at Blurrg, looked at the translucent curled up shrimp, and at Blurrg again.
Blurrg stepped forward slowly, sniffing cautiously.
The creature tried to mimic the sniff—then hiccuped void sparkles.
quiet void-croak
The portal snapped shut behind it with a clap of thunder.
All parties stared at each other in confusion.
Nervura: “…Is it dangerous?”
Smuggler: “PLEASE DON’T LET IT TOUCH ME!”
Blurrg: squints… sniffs again… interested croak
Plurrg wiggled, bumping into Blurrg’s leg—glowing brighter, like a tiny lantern of friendship.
That was enough.
Blurrg gently leaned down and nudged him toward their food crates.
Decision made.
Nervura sighed—but her smile betrayed her.
“Alright. Fine. Let’s bring it home.”
Blurrg and the Purple Blurrg wiggled with their tails.
“But you have to tell Blurrgina!”
The two Crawgs were already on their way to the portal, while Nervura still tried to comprehend what happened.
“And he needs a Name!”

Back at the Swamp

Nazmir’s humid twilight wrapped around them as they returned.
The crawg burrow was full of chittering and splashes until… Purple Blurrg glowed.
All small crawglings gasped dramatically and immediately swarmed him.
They poked his glow spots. They climbed him like a warm tree. They squeaked when he pulsed brighter.

Blurgette peeked from behind her rock, eyes wide.
When purple Blurrg glowed softly in greeting, she shyly offered her favorite stone.
He accepted with reverence.
Blurg stomped forward, huffing.
“Who is THIS glow-stick?!”
Purple Blurrg turned red just a moment—nervous.
Blurrgina slid beside Blurg and smacked him with her tail.
“Play nice,” she hissed.
Then she mustered the newcomer, walking in a spiraling circle around purple Blurrg, scrutinizing his stance, glow, rhythm.
Finally— Approval nod. And purple Blurrg glowed pink with joy. That night…
Nervura sat beside the campfire, adjusting her traps.
Blurrg curled up beside her, watching purple Blurrg fall asleep under a pile of baby crawglings.
The void glow dimmed gently, pulsing like a warm heartbeat.
Nervura whispered: “Where do you think he came from?”
Blurrg blinked slowly. Then, softly: “Doesn’t matter. Crawg now.” soft, protective croak
Nervura patted his head. “You have a big heart, little warrior.”
Blurrg puffed proudly. “We should call him Plurrg.”
Blurrg nodded with a relaxed huff and curled in tighter next to her, ready for sleep.
Somewhere in the swamp, a butterfly fluttered past.
Plurrg’s glow brightened in his sleep.
And in the dark, mysterious whispers echoed softly— not threatening, but curious.
A new family member.
A new story.
A new glow in Nazmir’s endless night.
